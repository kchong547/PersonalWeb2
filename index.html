Who am I?

My name is Katherine and I'm happy our paths are crossing even for a brief moment.

In 2022, I finished pursuing my Bachelor or Science in Computer Science at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA).

Though I've taken a small break from software development, I'm coming back into the scene with a fresh mind and a newfound interest in the technological world. I find it very hard to escape from my love for creating and being involved in imaginative processes; what better place to grow and challenge myself than in tech? I've made the most out of this period of my life and come back a much more motivated and stronger individual. I cannot wait to let my tenacity and curiosity shine through in the next things to come.

Currently, I'm interested in full-stack development. No matter what I do, I will always be happiest when I blend my creative abilities and my interested in tech together to create something new and exciting!

Leading the change

Association of Computing Machinery

ACM at UCLA is the largest computer science student organization at UCLA consisting of 8 subcommittees that each focus on a different topic related to tech and computer science, such as cybersecurity, diversity in tech, and artificial intelligence.

I served as the External Vice President to the board overlooking the entire organzation during the 2020-2021 term.

Here is a brief list of my responsibilities:

  • Maintained marketing strategies, social media, finances, corporate relations, and cross-organization partnerships along with my 8 person team.
  • Communicated with corporate representatives and recruiters from companies such as Google, Meta, and much more.
  • Incorporated new diversity and inclusion discussion in annual CS Town Hall, where students and faculty discussed ways to improve the CS curriculum and better support the student body.
  • Designed and lobbied for a cost-friendly sponsorship tier dedicated for start-ups for the UCLA CS Department Affiliates Program.
  • Oversaw initiatives from 8 committees, including new branding guidelines for the organization.
  • Worked with Moonshots initiative to foster diversity and inclusion awareness and implement new, student-led ideas.

exploretech.la

exploretech.la is a student-led organization that aims to expose underserved Title 1 high school students in LAUSD to opportunities in technology. This organization hosts an annual, day-long outreach event full of panels, workshops, and interactive activities for students to take part in.

In 2019-2020, this is what I accomplished as External Director:

  • Led a team of 4 that handled corporate partnerships, finances, marketing, and high school outreach.
  • Secured over $17,000 in funding via UCLA funding applications, corporate sponsorships, and department budgeting, leading to a ~55% in budget.
  • Established partnerships with companies such as Microsoft, Google, Capcom, and more.
  • Gathered over 200 students to attend the event.

From 2020-2022, I created and ran workshops on these topics:

  • Game Development
  • AR/VR
  • UI/UX
  • Python Turtles
